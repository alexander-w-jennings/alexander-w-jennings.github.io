Variables of Extraction

Listed below are the five variables involved in the brewing process, known as extraction.

Water Temperature
The temperature of the water can dramatically impact the extraction process. As a general rule, the hotter the water is, the more quickly the coffee will be extracted. The ideal temperature range for most brewing methods is between 195°F to 205°F.
Agitation
Agitation is the process of intentionally stirring or disrupting the coffee grounds with water during the brewing process. The more that a coffee is agitated (through pressure, water flow, or manual stirring), the more the coffee is extracted.
Grind Size
The size of the coffee grinds will impact the extraction process as well. The larger the grind size, the less surface area there is to extract. Conversely, the smaller the grind size, the more surface area there is for the water to extract
Contact Time
The amount of time that the coffee is suspended in the water will directly affect the extraction process. Generally, the longer that coffee is suspended in water, the more extraction will take place. It's important to note that contact time is typically a result of variables such as grind size. If the coffee is a finer grind, then the water cannot pass through as quickly, resulting in a longer contact time.
Brew Ratio
The coffee-to-water ratio is an important component to extraction. The acceptable range for brewing ratios within most percolation/pourover methods is 1:15 - 1:18. For espresso, the typical ratio is 1:2 - 1:3.
